Building on the original **mobile UX** of the [streaming news app](../welt-tv-app-handoff), the next step was translating the product to the **big screen** in the living room. Instead of redesigning the experience from scratch, the goal was to extend the existing product architecture to TV platforms while preserving familiar interaction patterns.

```small
The resulting TV application brings the core functionality of the [mobile and tablet product](../welt-tv-app-handoff) to Apple TV, Android TV, Samsung TV, Fire TV and Roku. It includes a hero-driven home screen, program detail pages, a continuous livestream, search and essential settings - all adapted for remote navigation, lean-back viewing and large-screen readability.
```

```mockup type="tv" bgColor="#F5F5F7" image="assets/vr/TV - 3.png"
```

## Designing for TV required adapting the interaction model rather than reinventing the product.

Touch-driven navigation was translated into a **focus-based remote interface**, while the content architecture of the existing app remained largely intact.

```small
The home screen highlights current programming through a large hero stage and editorial content rows. From there, users can enter program detail pages, browse topics or immediately start the livestream: maintaining the same editorial hierarchy already familiar from the [mobile experience](../welt-tv-app-handoff).
```

![assets/vr/TV - 4.png]

### Core TV Experience

- Hero-based home screen highlighting current programming and editorial picks
- Continuous livestream as the primary entry point into the product
- Detail pages for programs, topics and editorial video content
- Search for shows, topics and videos across the catalog
- Basic account and app settings
- Focus-based navigation optimized for remote control and lean-back viewing

```mockup type="tv" bgColor="#F5F5F7" image="assets/vr/TV-4.png"
```

While the TV application focuses on extending the existing product to the living room, the project also explored how the same content ecosystem could  **evolve on emerging spatial platforms**.

## Exploring VisionOS and Immersive News Experiences

On Apple Vision Pro, the concept shifts from a screen-based interface to a **spatial news environment**. Instead of a fixed layout, the livestream becomes a floating focal point surrounded by contextual information layers and immersive environments.

![/assets/vr/Video VisionOS.png]

![https://f33qiggog7gxjoua.public.blob.vercel-storage.com/projects/video-visionos-mmjhl4dg-961da1.webp]

```small
Users can watch news while standing inside **360° environments** captured from real locations: city squares during elections, reconstruction sites after natural disasters, or scientific expeditions. Documentary formats expand this concept further by offering fully **immersive storytelling** spaces.
```

- Floating live broadcast window with contextual data panels
- 360° environments from news locations (cities, events, crisis zones)
- Immersive documentary worlds: oceans, wildlife habitats, engineering sites
- Spatial article layers with charts, maps and timelines
- Ambient environments during ad breaks to keep viewers engaged

Together, these explorations outline two complementary directions: a production-ready TV application that scales the existing mobile product to living-room platforms, and a forward-looking spatial concept that rethinks how live news, data and documentary storytelling could be experienced in immersive environments.
