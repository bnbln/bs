Creating invoices on a smartphone – in practice, that's a risk workflow: under time pressure, between appointments, with rising regulatory demands. Since 2026, B2B e-invoicing has been mandatory in Germany. For small businesses and freelancers, a real hurdle – and the moment where poor UX causes real damage.

# Not a Form App, But a Guided Workflow.

## The Problem Wasn't a UI Problem
What users described was uncertainty. Is this invoice correct? Am I missing something? Why was it rejected? Research with freelancers and small teams revealed the same pattern every time:

- Mandatory fields and status logic are unclear
- Errors are discovered too late – when corrections are already costly
- Trust doesn't come from data models, but from a visible, correct PDF

## Product Strategy: A Safe Workflow, Not a Form

I shaped Rechnungen+ from problem framing to interaction logic – strategy, IA, UX/UI, content design, and error microcopy. The core idea: validation built in from the start, not bolted on at export. Complex input was split into focused subflows, status became an active control with a transparent timeline, and an AI feature reduces uncertainty directly in the flow.

The central question was: how do you translate regulatory complexity into clear decision spaces without overwhelming or micromanaging users? The answer lay in the interplay of early validation, progressive disclosure, and trust through transparency.


## IA and Product Structure
Navigation was structured as a productive workspace: Search, Invoices, Templates, Products, Contacts, Assist, Analytics, Scanner, Account.
The invoicing workflow is modular and continuous:

- Select or create recipient
- Pull line items from products or add them manually
- Set details (date, due date, currency, invoice number)
- Add text blocks and attachments
- Control status and process stage (quote, invoice, reminder, cancellation)
- Generate, review, share, and version the PDF in history

## Outcome
Rechnungen+ delivers an end-to-end mobile workflow from data capture to export-ready e-invoice – with a clear focus on clarity in domain-heavy steps, safety through early validation, and speed through reuse and guided flows.

Complexity was not designed away, but segmented in a meaningful way
Domain logic, UI states, and data model were integrated consistently
Trust-critical areas (status, history, export) were designed explicitly
Scope was managed deliberately: focus on EN16931/ZUGFeRD, with advanced tax cases defined as the next stage.


# What I Took Away
Good UX in complex domains doesn't mean hiding complexity – it means segmenting it meaningfully. The real work wasn't in the screens, but in the decisions beneath them: when to validate, what to surface and when, where guidance ends and paternalism begins. Rechnungen+ is a workflow you can trust. That was the goal.
